Cyberpunk 2077: Core Game Mechanics, Playstyles, and Main Story Arc

Cyberpunk 2077 is an open-world game that combines first-person shooter (FPS) mechanics with deep role-playing game (RPG) elements. Below is a breakdown of the core game mechanics, playstyles, and main story arc:

I. Core Game Mechanics and Gameplay

Survival in Night City depends entirely on how you modify your body and equipment. The game was completely overhauled in Update 2.0 and beyond, bringing immense depth to its combat and progression systems.

1. Character Attributes and Progression

At the start of the game, you can choose from three Lifepaths: Nomad, Street Kid, or Corpo. This primarily affects your prologue storyline and unlocks unique dialogue options throughout the game.

The core attributes are divided into five dimensions, which determine the direction of your skill trees:

Body: Increases health and melee damage, and unlocks skills for shotguns, machine guns, and blunt force/unarmed combat.

Reflexes: Increases critical hit chance and mobility, while unlocking skills for assault rifles, submachine guns (SMGs), and highly stylish katana maneuvers.

Technical Ability: Determines armor value, enhances the effectiveness of grenades and health items, and is key to unlocking advanced crafting and increasing Cyberware capacity.

Intelligence: The traditional "mage" attribute, which determines netrunning (hacking) capabilities, offensive/defensive network options, and Quickhack damage.

Cool: Focuses on stealth, assassination, critical damage, and bonuses for precision weapons like sniper rifles and pistols.

2. Cyberware System

This is the soul of Cyberpunk 2077's mechanics. Players must visit a Ripperdoc to implant high-tech components into various parts of their body:

Operating System: You can choose to equip a Cyberdeck (for a netrunner playstyle) or sacrifice hacking abilities in exchange for a Sandevistan (time-slowing trigger) or a Berserk operating system (melee invulnerability and damage reduction).

Integumentary System & Legs: Implanting double-jump (Reinforced Tendons) vastly improves vertical exploration, or installing Optical Camo grants temporary invisibility.

Arms: Mantis Blades (high-frequency slicing), Gorilla Arms (powerful blunt attacks and forcing doors open), Monowire (mid-range slicing), or Projectile Launch System (arm-mounted micro-missiles).

3. The Three Ranged Weapon Archetypes

Ranged weapons in the game are divided into three main categories, each with its own unique combat mechanics:

Power Weapons: Traditional ballistic weapons. Their bullets can ricochet off hard surfaces to strike enemies hiding behind cover.

Tech Weapons: Require charging before firing. Their rounds have incredible penetrative power, allowing players to snipe enemies directly through walls and cover.

Smart Weapons: Utilize self-homing ammunition. Paired with hand implants, the bullets can curve around obstacles to track targets within your field of view.

II. Main Story Arc

Cyberpunk 2077 tells a story about "survival, destiny, and the price of success."

1. The Deadly Heist (Prologue)

The protagonist V (Vincent or Valerie) is a mercenary on the lowest rungs of Night City's underworld. Seeking to make a name for themselves, V and their close friend Jackie Welles take on a high-stakes, legendary heist: infiltrating the high-end hotel owned by the mega-conglomerate Arasaka to steal a mysterious, experimental biochip—the Relic.

By terrible coincidence, the duo witnesses a patricidal tragedy within the Arasaka empire: Yorinobu Arasaka, the eldest son, strangles the ruler Saburo Arasaka to death with his own hands, pinning the blame on assassins. V and Jackie are hunted as they flee, and Jackie dies of his severe wounds. To prevent data corruption from a failing containment suit, V is forced to slot the Relic into their own neural socket. Afterward, the fixer Dexter DeShawn, seeking self-preservation, shoots V in the head at a motel and dumps the body in a junkyard.

2. Symbiosis and Struggle of the Soul (Development)

Against all odds, the Relic activates after V's brain death, using nanotechnology to repair V's brain damage and saving their life. But the cost is brutal: stored on the chip is the digital consciousness (Engram) of Johnny Silverhand, the legendary rockerboy and terrorist who blew up Arasaka Tower 50 years ago.

Once initiated, the process is irreversible. Johnny's consciousness begins to eat away at V's mind like terminal cancer, overwriting V's own memories. The Ripperdoc Viktor tells V that their body has only a few weeks of life remaining, after which they will be completely overwritten by Johnny.

To survive, V scrambles across Night City's various factions, seeking a way to extract the chip:

They kidnap Anders Hellman, the creator of the Relic.

They try to contact Alt Cunningham (Johnny's former girlfriend), a legendary AI beyond the Blackwall, through the Voodoo Boys.

During this journey, V builds alliances with people like the Nomad Panam, Detective River, and braindance technician Judy, while slowly uncovering the truth behind Johnny Silverhand's arrogant rebellion. From their initial violent struggle for control and mutual hostility, V and Johnny gradually transition into an empathetic, extraordinary symbiosis.

3. Decisions at Arasaka Tower (Endgame)

As V's body nears total collapse, all leads point to a single final solution: storming the heart of Arasaka Tower to reach Mikoshi—Arasaka's digital fortress used to imprison and analyze human souls—and utilizing its advanced systems to separate their minds.

Depending on the player's side-quest decisions and affinity rating, V will face different choices regarding their allies in the end:

A Desperate Gamble: Fight your way through with the Aldecaldos nomad family (The Star Ending).

Compromise and Surrender: Trust Hanako Arasaka, making a soul-selling deal with the Arasaka Corporation (The Devil Ending).

Going with an Old Friend: Hand body control to Johnny, letting him and his old partner Rogue storm Arasaka Tower once more (The Sun / Temperance Ending).

An Eye for an Eye (Hidden Ending): Drag no friends down with you, going in completely alone with just a gun to storm Arasaka Tower head-on (Don't Fear The Reaper).

In the digital realm of Mikoshi, V finally meets Alt Cunningham, but fate plays a classic cyberpunk trick: because V's physical body has suffered too much structural damage, even if Johnny's consciousness is successfully extracted, V will only have six months left to live. However, if the body is surrendered to Johnny completely, Johnny can live a full, healthy life.

Leave your body to Johnny while you become a mere stream of data in cyberspace, or return to a physical form that has only six months left to live—whether to go out in a brief blaze of glory as a legend of Night City, or run away with those you love. This ultimate choice rests entirely in the player's hands.
